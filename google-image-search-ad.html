The Challenge: Fragmentation at Scale

Google's internal engineering teams were using a suite of 12 disparate tools to manage code deployment. This fragmentation forced engineers to context-switch constantly, leading to:

  • High Error Rates: Critical deployment warnings were often missed in the noise of unorganized logs.
  • Lost Productivity: Engineers spent average 2 hours/week just navigating between tools.

"How might we unify the deployment workflow into a 'Single Pane of Glass' without overcomplicating the interface for power users?"

Discovery & Insight

I led a series of contextual inquiries with 15 engineers across different product areas (Search, Cloud, YouTube). We discovered a critical insight:

Insight 1: Trust > Simplicity

Engineers didn't want a "simple" UI; they wanted a dense, data-rich UI they could trust. Hiding complexity created anxiety.

Insight 2: Text is UI

For developers, logs *are* the interface. Standard UI components often got in the way of raw text parsing.

The Solution: Modular Density

We moved away from the standard Material Design "card" layout (which had too much whitespace) and developed a "High Density" table system.

1. The "Actionable" Status Bar

Instead of static traffic lights (Red/Green), we made status indicators interactive filters. Clicking "Failed" immediately filtered the log stream to relevant errors.

UI Detail: Interactive Filters

2. Unified Command Center

We introduced a persistent command line at the bottom of the UI, allowing power users to execute scripts without leaving the GUI context.

40%

15%

92%

Positive sentiment (CSAT) from the pilot engineering group.

Reflection & Learning

This project taught me that "User Experience" in enterprise tools isn't about delight—it's about predictability and efficiency. The biggest challenge was aligning 12 different stakeholder teams who all "owned" a piece of the old workflow. By focusing on shared data (the logs) rather than the UI initially, we built consensus from the bottom up.
